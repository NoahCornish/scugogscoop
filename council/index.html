How it works

How Scugog Council is structured

Scugog voters elect a Mayor and Regional Councillor through Township-wide votes. Residents also elect one councillor to represent each of the municipality’s five wards.

The Mayor and Regional Councillor sit on Durham Regional Council in addition to serving locally. Regional Council is responsible for services and policy areas delivered across Durham Region, while Scugog Council addresses matters under the Township’s jurisdiction.

Township Council considers local budgets, taxation, roads, recreation, parks, municipal facilities, planning applications, development proposals and local by-laws.

Council decisions are made collectively through motions and recorded votes. Individual members cannot independently direct Township operations.

Contacting an elected official

Residents may contact Council members directly using their official municipal email addresses and telephone numbers. Constituents commonly contact councillors about local services, neighbourhood concerns, upcoming agenda items and decisions before Council.

Formal correspondence related to a public meeting may become part of the Township’s public record. Residents seeking to make a formal delegation should consult the Township’s meeting and Clerk’s Department information before the meeting deadline.
