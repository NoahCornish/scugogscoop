Questions worth asking

Good accountability is specific.

What was purchased?

Scope, deliverables, contract term and procurement method.

What changed?

Measurable outputs, secured funding, completed work or adopted recommendations.

Was it good value?

Clearly label this conclusion as analysis or opinion—not as an established fact.
